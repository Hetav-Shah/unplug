Two smoking-gun features completely outside YZ range. Sign flip in corr(Tx,Tz). The Fy channel dominates with 36× the variance of Fz — physically impossible for YZ separation.
Smoking-gun signals for 45.csv → XZ

std(Fy)/std(Fz) = 36.1 vs YZ range [0.24 – 0.97]. In YZ separation, Fy barely fluctuates because Y is not the slip plane. Here Fy is 36× noisier than Fz — Y-axis force variability completely dominates, consistent with XZ separation where Y becomes the free direction.

corr(Tx, Tz) = +0.182 vs YZ range [−0.92, −0.90]. The strong negative Tx/Tz coupling is the structural fingerprint of YZ-plane torque transmission. In 45.csv the sign has completely flipped — this coupling is physically absent.
